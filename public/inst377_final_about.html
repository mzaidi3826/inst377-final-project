About This Website

Project Overview

The Email Risk Checker is a dynamic web application created for the INST377: Dynamic Web Applications course. The goal of this project is to help users understand how vulnerable an email address may be to fraud, abuse, or data breaches by leveraging publicly available risk-analysis APIs.

The Problem

Email accounts are a primary target for cyberattacks. Compromised email addresses can lead to identity theft, phishing attacks, financial fraud, and unauthorized access to sensitive systems. Many individuals and organizations are unaware of how exposed their email addresses may be until a breach has already occurred.

Who This Is For

This project is designed around an imaginary government organization called the U.S. Department of Buses, which relies heavily on email for daily internal and external communication. The primary stakeholders include:

  • Employees who want to know if their email accounts are at risk
  • IT staff who need to identify vulnerable accounts proactively
  • Executives who need data-driven insights to support cybersecurity decisions

How It Works

Users enter an email address on the home page. The application sends the email to a custom backend API, which then queries the IPQualityScore Email Risk API. The response is analyzed and transformed into a simplified risk score and classification:

  • Low Risk – Minimal signs of abuse or fraud
  • Medium Risk – Some indicators of suspicious behavior
  • High Risk – Strong evidence of fraud, abuse, or misuse

The results are displayed in both text form and as a visual chart to make the information easy to understand.

Data & APIs Used

  • IPQualityScore Email API – Used to analyze email addresses for fraud risk, abuse history, and deliverability.
  • Public CSV Data – Sample government-style email addresses are loaded from a CSV file for testing and demonstration purposes.

Technology Stack

  • HTML & CSS for structure and styling
  • JavaScript for dynamic behavior and API interaction
  • Node.js & Express for the backend server
  • Chart.js for data visualization
  • Supabase (PostgreSQL) for data storage
  • Vercel for deployment

Project Goal

The goal of this application is not to definitively label an email address as “safe” or “unsafe,” but to provide users with actionable information that can help them make informed decisions about cybersecurity and risk prevention.
